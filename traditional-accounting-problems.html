Traditional Accounting Problems

Traditional accounting give low detailed information and lack the needed granularity to properly do cost analysis and, therefore, cost reduction.

Tradicional Accounting Systems

Most accounting systems are focused on mandatory state-demanded reports showing only large blocks of information totally misaligned with the organization’s business processes. Therefore, when it comes to calculate the cost of a product or service, traditional methodologies give inaccurate values, mostly because they lack the needed granularity and differentiation of information.

Often, such information is inaccurate because of wrong distribution of overhead costs. Correct distribution of overhead costs is truly essential since they have grown from being a minor share of the total costs to the major one.

Tradicional Accounting Problems

Tradicional Accounting methodologies have the following issues:

  • Imprecise: traditional methodologies give inaccurate values, mostly because they lack the needed granularity and differentiation of information and also because they assign costs based on averages;
  • Untraceability: most accounting systems are focused on mandatory state-demanded reports showing only large blocks of information totally misaligned with the organization’s business processes, products and services;
  • Outdated: there are new costing methodologies (ABC and TDABC) that overcome the Tradicional Accounting problems. However, they require a lot of expertise and are normally supported by very expensive and complex software solutions.
